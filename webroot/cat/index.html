The Cat's Plate

October 12, 2025 — Retold folktale

Long ago in Edo, an antiques dealer roamed the streets in search of hidden treasures. He was known for his sharp eyes, spotting valuable pieces where others saw only junk.

One afternoon, weary from his search, he stopped at a teahouse. Three cats were eating scraps of fish from a rough wooden floor. Yet what caught his attention was not the cats, but the small bowl they ate from. Though chipped and dirty, it was unmistakably an antique that could fetch a fortune.

The dealer smiled and struck up conversation with the owner of the stall. Pretending to admire the animals, he asked, “Would you sell me one of these cats? I will give you three gold coins.”

The stall keeper laughed at the strange request, but agreed. Cats were common, and three coins was generous. The dealer paid and scooped up the animal, stroking it fondly.

As he prepared to leave, he reached down and lifted the cracked bowl. “Of course,” he said, “I will take its dish as well. The poor creature will not eat from another.”

The stall keeper stopped him at once. “That bowl is no ordinary dish. I cannot part with it. In truth, it is worth hundreds of gold coins.”

The dealer widened his eyes in feigned surprise. “If you know its value, why feed your cats with it?”

With a wry smile, the stall keeper replied, “Because with that bowl, I often sell an ordinary cat for three gold coins.”
